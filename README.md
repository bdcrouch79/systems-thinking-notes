# systems-thinking-notes

**A collection of mental models, principles, and frameworks for understanding systems.**

---

## What This Is

This repository captures how systems are understood — not just how they are built.

It focuses on:

- patterns that repeat across domains  
- principles that guide good design  
- mental models that simplify complexity  

---

## Why This Exists

Most people approach problems at the surface level.

Systems thinking goes deeper:

- identifying structure beneath behavior  
- understanding cause vs. symptom  
- designing with intent instead of reacting  

This repository captures that layer of thinking.

---

## What You’ll Find

- mental models for analyzing systems  
- principles for designing better structures  
- notes on failure patterns and inefficiencies  
- early ideas that shape future implementations  

---

## Diagrams

The `/diagrams/` directory contains visual representations of system behavior and structure.

These are used to:

- clarify thinking  
- expose hidden relationships  
- simplify complex interactions  

---

## Core Ideas

- systems produce what they are designed to produce  
- complexity hides inefficiency  
- structure determines behavior  
- feedback loops drive outcomes  

---

## Relationship to Other Work

This repository supports more applied work:

- ideas are explored here  
- designs are sketched in architecture repositories  
- execution happens in product systems  

---

## Philosophy

Understand before optimizing  
Simplify before scaling  
Design before building
